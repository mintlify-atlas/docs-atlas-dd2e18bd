`ft viz` renders a full ANSI terminal dashboard that gives you a visual overview of your entire bookmark collection — patterns, habits, top voices, and content composition — all in one place.

## Running the dashboard

```bash
ft viz
```

The dashboard renders immediately from your local SQLite index. No network requests are made.

<Note>
`ft viz` requires a built search index. Run `ft index` first if you haven't already.
</Note>

## What the dashboard shows

The dashboard is composed of several sections, rendered in sequence:

### Hidden gems

Authors you've bookmarked exactly once who wrote long, substantive posts. Easy to forget; surfaced here so they're not lost.

### Time capsules

Your oldest bookmarked posts, one per year. Shows what you were saving years ago and what still held up.

### Who you listen to

A ranked bar chart of your 20 most-bookmarked authors, with bar length proportional to bookmark count. Gradient-colored from blue (lower counts) to coral (highest count).

### Categories

A bar chart of your bookmark categories (e.g., `research`, `tool`, `technique`). Only appears after you've run `ft classify`.

### Domains

A bar chart of subject domains (e.g., `ai`, `devtools`, `finance`). Only appears after domain classification.

### Rhythm

A sparkline of monthly bookmarking activity followed by per-month bar charts. Shows how your bookmarking cadence has changed over time.

### Weekly pulse

A braille dot chart and bar chart showing which days of the week you bookmark most.

### Daily arc

A 24-column vertical bar chart showing the hour of day when you reach for the bookmark button. Annotates your peak hour and quietest hour.

### Where links lead

The top 12 external domains found in your bookmarked tweets — sites your saved content links to most often (excludes `x.com` and `t.co`).

### Composition

A stacked bar showing the proportion of bookmarks that contain media, external links, or are text-only.

### Rising

Authors whose bookmarks all come from your most recent month — new voices in your collection.

### Fingerprint

A summary box with key stats: average bookmark text length, percentage with media, long-tail author proportion, and your top voice. Also shows which authors appeared in your most recent sync session.

## Text-based alternatives

If you prefer plain text output, two commands give you the same category and domain data as tables:

```bash
# Category distribution with percentages
ft categories

# Subject domain distribution with percentages
ft domains
```

Example output:

```
  research          312  (28.4%)
  tool              218  (19.8%)
  technique         187  (17.0%)
  opinion           143  (13.0%)
  launch             98   (8.9%)
  security           72   (6.6%)
  commerce           68   (6.2%)
```

<Tip>
`ft viz` is designed for a terminal with true-color (24-bit) support and a monospace font. If colors look off, check that your terminal emulator supports RGB color — most modern terminals (iTerm2, Warp, Kitty, Windows Terminal) do.
</Tip>
